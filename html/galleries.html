Nomadic Burial Finds

Echo Fang Pendant

Material: Crane ulna with five finger holes; mouthpiece plugged with resin.
Size/Weight/Shape: Length 31.2 cm; bore 7.3 mm; 72 g.
Physical Properties: Smooth leather braid and bronze fittings with slight patina
Estimated Age: Han to Six Dynasties (2nd–4th c. CE) by organology and residue dating.
Preservation State: Hairline crack near foot bound with silk thread in antiquity.
Location Found: Dry cave cache in the Qinling foothills with other instruments.
Likely Purpose: Ceremonial melodies and pastoral calls; timbre described in legend as ‘voices of ancestors.’
Evidence of Use: Lip wear and resin polish; interior shows cleaning scrape marks.
Manufacture Clues: Holes bored with tapered awl then tuned by knife scraping; end‑stop of pine resin and beeswax.
Found With: Fragmentary panpipes and a small bell.
Burial or Habitat Context: Cave microclimate buffered humidity, preventing collagen loss.
Symbolism: Bone instruments bridge human and avian realms, carrying prayers upward.
Comparison: Comparable to bone flutes from Jiahu in concept though far later in date.
